Weighbridges are one of the most critical pieces of infrastructure in industries like agriculture, mining, quarrying, and logistics — yet most people outside these sectors have never heard of them. Let me break down how they work.

## What is a Weighbridge?

A weighbridge (also called a truck scale) is a large industrial scale built into the ground that allows entire vehicles — loaded trucks, trailers, or rail cars — to be weighed while remaining on the scale platform.

They're used to:
- Measure commodity quantities for buying/selling
- Enforce axle load limits on roads
- Track inventory entering or leaving facilities
- Ensure compliance with regulatory requirements

## Core Components

### 1. The Platform (Deck)

The weighing platform is typically made of steel or reinforced concrete. Standard truck scales in Nigeria are:
- **Length**: 12–20 meters
- **Width**: 3–3.5 meters
- **Capacity**: 40–120 tonnes

The platform can be installed:
- **Pit-mounted**: Flush with the ground (requires civil works, better for high-traffic areas)
- **Surface-mounted**: Above ground level (faster installation, portable options available)

### 2. Load Cells

Load cells are the heart of the system — they convert mechanical force into electrical signals.

**Types used in weighbridges:**

- **Compression load cells**: Mounted under the platform corners, they measure force as the deck compresses them
- **Shear beam load cells**: Used in many modern designs for better accuracy

**How they work:**

```
Truck weight → Compresses platform → Platform compresses load cells
→ Load cells deform (strain) → Strain gauges detect change in resistance
→ Bridge circuit converts resistance change to voltage
→ Electronics convert voltage to weight reading
```

A typical 4-module weighbridge uses 8–16 load cells for redundancy and accuracy.

### 3. Junction Box

The junction box sums the signals from all load cells and sends a single output to the weight indicator. It also allows individual load cell trimming for calibration.

### 4. Weight Indicator / Display

The weight indicator is the electronic "brain" that:
- Receives the load cell signal
- Applies calibration factors
- Displays the weight
- Stores transaction data
- Communicates with software systems

Modern indicators support:
- RS-232/RS-485 serial communication
- Ethernet/WiFi connectivity
- USB data export
- Printer output

### 5. Software System

Most commercial weighbridges now integrate with weighing software that provides:
- Transaction recording with timestamps
- Vehicle registration tracking
- Tare weight storage (empty vehicle weight)
- Report generation
- Integration with ERP systems

## The Calibration Process

Calibration ensures the scale reads accurately. In Nigeria, weighbridges must be calibrated by certified bodies.

### What happens during calibration:

1. **Zero test**: Confirm the scale reads zero with no load
2. **Linearity test**: Apply known test weights at multiple intervals
3. **Repeatability test**: Apply the same weight multiple times
4. **Eccentricity test**: Place the same weight in different positions on the deck
5. **Certificate issue**: If within tolerance, a calibration certificate is issued

**Acceptable tolerances:**
- Class III scales: ±0.2% of applied load
- Legal-for-trade applications require verification by weights and measures authorities

## Common Problems We See in Nigeria

After servicing hundreds of weighbridges, here are the most common issues:

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Drifting readings | Temperature changes, worn load cells | Recalibrate, replace damaged cells |
| Unstable display | Electrical noise, poor earthing | Improve grounding, install filters |
| Negative readings | Damaged or overloaded cell | Replace load cell |
| Software errors | Power fluctuations | UPS installation, software update |

## Choosing the Right Weighbridge

If you're considering installing a weighbridge, key questions to answer:

1. **Maximum vehicle weight?** (determines platform capacity)
2. **Platform length?** (longest vehicle plus buffer)
3. **Environment?** (corrosive, dusty, temperature extremes)
4. **Connectivity needs?** (standalone vs. integrated system)
5. **Traffic volume?** (determines durability requirements)
