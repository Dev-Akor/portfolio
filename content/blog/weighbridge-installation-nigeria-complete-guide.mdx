## Who Needs a Weighbridge in Nigeria?

Before discussing installation, let's confirm if your business actually needs one. Weighbridges are essential for:

- **Agricultural processing** — cocoa, palm oil, cassava, soybean: buying and selling by weight
- **Quarrying and mining** — sand, granite, gravel, coal, limestone
- **Cement and building materials** — tracking production and dispatch
- **Logistics and haulage** — billing by load weight, preventing overloading
- **Steel and manufacturing** — raw material intake and finished goods dispatch
- **Government agencies** — road weight enforcement and customs
- **Rice mills and grain storage** — commodity measurement for trade

If your business regularly moves large quantities of bulk material and accuracy in quantity measurement directly affects your profit or regulatory compliance, a weighbridge is a business necessity, not a luxury.

## Step 1: Site Assessment

### Choosing the Right Location

A weighbridge is permanent infrastructure. Location selection is irreversible, so get it right.

**Critical siting factors:**

**Traffic flow** — The weighbridge must fit into your site's vehicle movement pattern. Trucks should approach and exit the scale without reversing, tight turns, or obstructing other operations.

**Ground conditions** — The sub-soil must have sufficient bearing capacity to support fully loaded trucks (up to 120+ tonnes including the platform itself). Soft, waterlogged, or unstable ground requires additional civil works.

**Access to utilities** — The control room needs electricity (at minimum a 220V single-phase supply, ideally 3-phase for industrial installations). Consider proximity to your generator if power outages are common.

**Future expansion** — Can the approach road accommodate longer trucks in the future? Plan for 20–25 metre platform lengths even if you start shorter.

**Drainage** — Pit-mounted weighbridges require excellent drainage to prevent flooding. Surface-mounted (above-ground) designs are better suited to areas with high water tables.

## Step 2: Choosing Your Weighbridge Type

### Pit-Mounted vs. Surface-Mounted

This is the most fundamental decision in the installation process.

**Pit-Mounted (In-Ground) Weighbridge**

The platform sits flush with the ground. Trucks drive onto it at road level.

✅ Better for high-traffic installations  
✅ Easier for drivers to navigate  
✅ More aesthetically integrated into the site  
✅ Safer for loading operations alongside the scale  

⚠️ Requires significant civil/excavation works  
⚠️ Drainage management is critical  
⚠️ Longer installation time (4–8 weeks for civil works)  
⚠️ Higher initial civil cost  

**Surface-Mounted (Above-Ground) Weighbridge**

The platform sits on top of the ground with approach ramps on each end.

✅ Faster to install (1–2 weeks)  
✅ Lower civil cost  
✅ Better drainage — water doesn't pool  
✅ Easier load cell access for maintenance  
✅ Portable options available for temporary sites  

⚠️ Ramps require more space  
⚠️ Approach angle can challenge heavily laden trucks  
⚠️ Not ideal for very high-volume operations  

**Our recommendation** for most Nigerian installations: surface-mounted. The combination of faster installation, lower cost, and easier maintenance suits most operational contexts. We recommend pit-mounted only for high-volume installations where continuous operation justifies the additional civil investment.

### Capacity Selection

| Business Type | Recommended Capacity |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Small farms / mills | 30–40 tonnes |
| Mid-size quarry / logistics | 60–80 tonnes |
| Heavy industry / port operations | 100–120 tonnes |

Always add 20–30% buffer to your expected maximum vehicle weight. A 60-tonne rated truck is typically loaded to 50–55 tonnes; a 60-tonne scale leaves no margin.

### Platform Length

| Truck Type | Minimum Platform Length |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Single axle trucks (9–12 tonnes) | 9 metres |
| Multi-axle trucks (33–54 tonnes) | 12 metres |
| Articulated vehicles | 18–24 metres |

## Step 3: Civil Works

Civil works are the most time-consuming and cost-intensive part of a weighbridge installation. This is where many projects go wrong.

### Foundation Requirements

The weighbridge foundation must:
- Distribute load evenly across all load cells
- Be perfectly level (maximum tolerance: 3mm across the full length)
- Have adequate depth for local soil conditions (typically 600mm–1200mm)
- Include drainage channels to prevent water ingress

### The Pitfall Most Contractors Miss

Many civil contractors in Nigeria have not built weighbridge foundations before. They build them like a regular concrete slab — which is wrong. A weighbridge foundation has specific requirements for:

1. **Reinforcement placement** — particularly around load cell mounting points
2. **Surface flatness** — must be machine-levelled, not hand-finished
3. **Curing time** — at minimum 28 days before the steel deck is placed
4. **Pocket dimensions** — must match the specific load cell configuration

## Step 4: Load Cell Selection

Load cells are the measurement heart of the system. Quality matters enormously.

### Load Cell Types

**Compression load cells** (column type) — the most common for weighbridges. Simple, robust, and proven in harsh environments.

**Bending beam / shear beam cells** — used in lower-capacity applications. Less common in truck scales.

### Key Specifications

| Parameter | What to Look For |
|-----------|----------------|
| Accuracy class | OIML Class C3 or better |
| Capacity per cell | Maximum platform capacity ÷ number of cells × 1.5 |
| IP rating | IP68 (fully waterproof) — essential for Nigeria's wet seasons |
| Temperature range | –20°C to +70°C minimum |
| Material | Stainless steel preferred over painted steel |
| Certification | OIML certificate, calibration certificate |

### How Many Load Cells?

A standard 12-metre, 4-module platform uses 8 load cells (2 per module end). Longer platforms use more modules and more cells. More load cells = better redundancy but also more calibration complexity.

## Step 5: Electronics and Software

### Weight Indicator / Controller

The weight indicator is the electronic brain that:
- Receives signals from all load cells via the junction box
- Applies calibration factors and algorithms
- Displays the weight reading
- Controls the printer
- Communicates with external software

**What to look for:**

- **Resolution**: 1kg or 0.5kg divisions for trade use
- **Display**: Bright sunlight-readable LED/LCD (critical in Nigerian outdoor conditions)
- **Connectivity**: RS-232, RS-485, Ethernet, USB
- **Power**: Wide input range (180V–240V AC) plus battery backup for power outages
- **Certifications**: OIML, CE

### Weighbridge Management Software

Standalone indicators are fine for simple operations. Most businesses need management software for:

- Capturing vehicle registration, driver, and product information
- Linking inbound and outbound weighments (net weight calculation)
- Generating weighment tickets with transaction details
- Report generation (daily, weekly, monthly tonnage summaries)
- Integration with inventory or ERP systems

## Step 6: Regulatory Compliance and Calibration

### Weights and Measures Requirements in Nigeria

Weighbridges used for **trade** (buying and selling by weight) are regulated by the Nigerian Weights and Measures Act. Requirements include:

- Calibration by an approved verification authority
- Calibration certificate displayed or available on request
- Regular re-verification (typically annually)
- Use of approved test weights for verification

### The Calibration Process

1. **Zero test** — confirm empty scale reads 0.0 kg
2. **Span calibration** — apply certified test weights across the range
3. **Linearity check** — weights tested at 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% of capacity
4. **Repeatability test** — same load applied 3+ times
5. **Eccentricity test** — same load in different positions on the deck
6. **Certificate issue** — if within tolerance, calibration certificate issued

**Calibration tolerance for OIML Class III:** ±0.1% of applied load at maximum capacity.

## Step 7: Commissioning and Training

After installation and calibration, the commissioning phase ensures everything works as intended:

**Hardware checks:**
- All cable connections verified
- Load cell outputs balanced
- Indicator display tested
- Printer connected and tested
- Network connectivity confirmed (if applicable)

**Software setup:**
- Database configured with your product categories and vehicle list
- User accounts created with appropriate permissions
- Report templates configured
- Backup system tested

**Operator training:**
- Scale operation and daily zero check
- Transaction recording and ticket printing
- End-of-day reporting
- Basic troubleshooting
