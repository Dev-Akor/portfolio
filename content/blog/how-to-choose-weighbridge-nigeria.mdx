A weighbridge is not a commodity purchase. Two machines that look identical on paper can differ enormously in reliability, accuracy, and total cost of ownership over five to ten years. Making the wrong choice means years of expensive maintenance, operational disruption, and measurement disputes with customers and suppliers.

This buyer's guide will help you ask the right questions and make a decision you won't regret.

---

## Start With Your Operation, Not the Equipment

Before comparing specifications, understand your own needs clearly. Answer these questions:

### 1. What is the heaviest vehicle you will weigh?

Not your average vehicle — your heaviest. Include a safety margin.

| Vehicle Type | Typical GVW (Gross Vehicle Weight) |
|-------------|-----------------------------------|
| Light truck (small tipper) | 8–12 tonnes |
| Medium truck (TATA, Sinotruk, MAN) | 25–33 tonnes |
| Heavy articulated truck | 45–54 tonnes |
| Road train (multi-trailer) | 80+ tonnes |

**Rule:** Choose a scale rated at minimum 120% of your heaviest expected vehicle weight.

### 2. How many vehicles will use the scale per day?

Traffic volume affects:
- Platform durability requirements (higher traffic = heavier deck structure)
- Speed of operation (high-volume sites need fast print-and-go systems)
- Software complexity (high volume means queuing, ticketing, and reporting matter more)

| Daily Traffic | Tier |
|--------------|------|
| Up to 30 trucks | Light duty |
| 30–100 trucks | Medium duty |
| 100+ trucks | Heavy duty / continuous |

### 3. What material are you weighing?

Corrosive, abrasive, or wet materials affect platform material selection and protection requirements. Cement dust, fertilizer, and maritime environments are particularly demanding.

### 4. Do you need permanent or temporary installation?

If your site is permanent (a factory, mill, or quarry), a fixed installation is almost always the right choice. If you're operating from a temporary location or need the scale to move between sites, portable axle weighpads or a relocatable surface-mounted scale makes more sense.

## Platform: Steel or Concrete Deck?

### Steel Deck Weighbridges

The most common choice for Nigerian installations.

✅ Faster to install  
✅ Easier to repair if damaged  
✅ Lighter — better for softer ground  
✅ Fully modular — can be extended  

⚠️ Surface can become slippery when wet (use chequer plate or grating)  
⚠️ Requires periodic anti-corrosion treatment  

### Concrete Deck Weighbridges

More common in Europe; increasingly available in Nigeria.

✅ Extremely durable surface  
✅ No rust issues  
✅ Better traction in all weather  

⚠️ Heavier — requires stronger foundation  
⚠️ Cannot be easily modified or extended  
⚠️ Cracking possible if foundation settles unevenly  
⚠️ Less available locally — higher import costs  

**Recommendation for Nigeria:** Steel deck. It's the dominant format, locally available, easier to repair, and well-suited to Nigerian site conditions.

## Load Cell Quality: The Most Important Specification Nobody Talks About

The platform is what you see. The load cells are what actually make the measurement. Quality here is everything.

### Brands to Look For

**Tier 1 (international, fully certified):**
- Mettler Toledo (Switzerland/USA)
- HBM/Hottinger Baldwin Messtechnik (Germany)
- Flintec (UK)
- Zemic (China — reputable, widely used)
- Anyload (Canada/China)

**What to avoid:**
- Uncertified generic cells with no traceable specification
- Cells without an OIML calibration certificate
- Cells without an IP68 waterproofing rating

### Why Cheap Load Cells Are a False Economy

A cheap load cell fails in 18–24 months under Nigerian conditions (heat, dust, moisture, occasional overloading). Replacement costs — technician travel, cell cost, calibration — often exceed the original savings.

A quality load cell from Zemic or Flintec, properly installed, should last 8–15 years.

## Electronics: What to Look for in a Weight Indicator

### The Indicator Matters More Than You Think

The weight indicator is the interface between your scale and your operations. A bad indicator causes:
- Frequent communication errors with software
- Display readability problems in sunlight
- Lost transaction data during power outages
- Slow print speeds that create truck queues

### Key Features Checklist

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Bright LED display | Readable in full Nigerian sunlight |
| Battery backup (UPS) | Continues operating during NEPA outages |
| Wide input voltage (180–250V) | Handles voltage fluctuations |
| RS-232/485 + Ethernet | Multiple connectivity options |
| Fast print speed | Reduces truck waiting time |
| Data logging | Backup if software fails |
| OIML approval | Legal for trade use |

### Recommended Brands

- Kern (Germany)
- Mettler Toledo ICS series
- Excell (Australia)
- Avery Weigh-Tronix
- Dibal (Spain)

## Software: How Complex Do You Need It?

### Option 1: Standalone Indicator (No Software)

The indicator displays the weight and prints a ticket. Weight, tare, net, date, time, ticket number.

**Good for:** Very small operations (under 20 trucks/day), single product, minimal reporting requirements.

### Option 2: Basic Weighbridge Software

A dedicated weighbridge management application on a PC or tablet. Records transactions, stores vehicle data, generates basic reports.

**Good for:** Most small to medium Nigerian businesses.

Features to look for:
- Vehicle registration database
- Driver capture
- Product/commodity coding
- Net weight calculation (gross minus tare)
- Daily transaction reports
- Data export (Excel/PDF)

### Option 3: Integrated Weighbridge System

Connects to your existing ERP (SAP, Sage, Odoo, QuickBooks) or logistics software. Vehicle data captured at entry, weight data posted automatically to inventory or billing systems.

**Good for:** Large operations where manual data entry is a significant cost or error source.

### Option 4: CCTV-Integrated System

Cameras capture the truck registration plate and cab photo at the time of weighing. The image is stored with the transaction record.

**Why it matters in Nigeria:** Eliminates driver fraud (same truck weighing twice, ticket falsification, ghost deliveries). Very common in cocoa, palm oil, and quarrying operations where there's cash on delivery.

## Pit-Mounted vs. Surface-Mounted: The Final Word

We covered this in our [installation guide](/blog/weighbridge-installation-nigeria-complete-guide), but here's the decision framework in brief:

**Choose pit-mounted if:**
- You have 80+ truck movements per day
- Your site layout demands ground-level access on both ends
- You have the civil budget and construction timeline to accommodate it

**Choose surface-mounted if:**
- You need the scale operational quickly
- Civil budget is constrained
- Your water table is high or drainage is problematic
- You might need to relocate the scale in the future

## Evaluating Suppliers in Nigeria

The equipment is only part of the purchase. The supplier relationship is equally important because:

1. You'll need them for calibration (annually at minimum)
2. You'll need them for maintenance and spare parts
3. You'll need emergency support when something goes wrong

### Questions to Ask Any Supplier

**About the equipment:**
- Where is the platform manufactured? (Nigeria, China, Europe?)
- What is the load cell brand and where can I verify the OIML certificate?
- What is the warranty period and what does it cover?
- What spare parts do you stock locally?

**About calibration:**
- Do you carry your own certified test weights?
- Can you show me the calibration certificates for your test weights?
- How quickly can you attend a calibration call?

**About support:**
- What is your emergency response time?
- Do you offer maintenance contracts?
- What happens if a load cell fails outside warranty?

**About their track record:**
- Can you provide references from similar installations?
- How long have you been operating in Nigeria?
- Do you have technicians based in my region?

## Total Cost of Ownership: The Right Way to Compare Quotes

When comparing quotes from different suppliers, the purchase price is only one factor.

### True 5-Year Cost Framework

| Cost Item | Low-Quality Equipment | Quality Equipment |
|-----------|----------------------|------------------|
| Purchase price | ₦5M | ₦8M |
| Installation | ₦300K | ₦300K |
| Calibration (5 years × ₦150K) | ₦750K | ₦750K |
| Load cell replacements (likely 2–3 failures) | ₦600K | ₦0 |
| Indicator repair/replacement | ₦200K | ₦50K |
| Downtime cost (5 operational days lost) | ₦500K+ | ₦50K |
| **5-Year Total** | **₦7.35M+** | **₦9.15M** |

The cheap option isn't cheap. The quality equipment costs less over time despite a higher initial price.

## Making the Decision

Here's a simple decision framework:

```
1. Determine maximum load capacity needed → Add 20% safety margin
2. Choose platform type (steel deck recommended)
3. Determine installation type (surface-mounted unless high volume)
4. Define software requirements (standalone → basic → integrated)
5. Assess supplier: equipment quality, test weight certification, support capability
6. Compare total cost of ownership, not purchase price alone
```
